Since 2012, the Sustainable Development Solutions Network & Columbia University's Center for Sustainable Development have surveyed people across the globe about their subjective wellbeing and perceived happiness.

The six factors that are hypothesized to contribute to happiness are: GDP per capita, social support, healthy life expectancy, freedom, generosity, and absence of corruption. Individuals rate their happiness based on an assessment of their own lives via the Cantril ladder scale, where they rank themselves a ladder of 10 steps, with higher numbers indicating greater happiness.
